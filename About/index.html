About Karen

Librarian. Storyteller.
Texan by birth. Mountain dweller by choice.

From the wide skies of West Texas and the Austin of another era to the Blue Ridge Mountains of North Carolina, Karen Hodges has spent a lifetime collecting stories shaped by place, people, faith, family, and memory.

West Texas Beginnings

Karen grew up in West Texas, where wide skies, small towns, windmills, bluebonnets, and family stories helped shape the way she sees the world. Long before she became a writer, she was learning to notice the details that make ordinary lives memorable.

Austin Years

Karen’s connection to Austin spans more than half a century. As a student at the University of Texas in the 1960s, she experienced the city during one of the most defining chapters in its history.

She was in the UT Tower on August 1, 1966, the day of the tragic Tower shooting. That firsthand connection to the campus, the era, and the emotional weight of the event gives special depth to her historical mystery, Clock Tower Connection.

Though the novel is fiction, its atmosphere grows from Karen’s lived memory of Austin, the University, and a moment in Texas history that was never forgotten.

A Life Among Books

Karen spent much of her professional life as a school librarian, surrounded by books, students, and curious minds. She helped young readers discover stories, ask questions, and see reading as a doorway into larger worlds.

Librarian by profession. Storyteller by nature.

Life in the Mountains

Today Karen lives in the mountains of western North Carolina. The ridges, trails, wildflowers, wildlife, and changing seasons have become part of her later-life story, adding a new landscape to a life still deeply rooted in Texas.

Whether writing fiction or memoir, Karen remains drawn to the stories people carry with them — the memories, places, and moments that stay long after the page is turned.

Why I Write

“I’ve always believed stories help us understand one another. Some stories entertain. Some preserve memories. Some help us make sense of where we’ve been. Whether I’m writing about Austin in the 1960s or sharing memories from my own life, I’m really exploring the people, places, and moments that stay with us long after the page is turned.”

Stories That Stay

Karen’s books may take different forms — a historical mystery set in 1960s Austin and a warm memoir about family, faith, aging, and gratitude — but both grow from the same belief: stories help us remember what matters.

After years spent helping students find meaningful books, Karen now writes stories of her own, preserving memories from history, family, and everyday life.

Facts tell, but stories stay.
